What is EntheoGen?

EntheoGen is a drug‑interaction comparison dataset specialising in intentional, ceremonial, underground, and naturalistic psychedelic use. It focuses on how entheogens interact with medications and other consciousness‑altering substances in real‑world patterns: ayahuasca ceremonies, multi‑medicine retreats, clinical protocols, and psychonautic practice.

Why build it?

Existing drug‑interaction tools are mostly designed for conventional pharmacy and emergency medicine. They often:

  • Ignore entheogens entirely, or collapse them into vague "recreational drug" categories.
  • Issue blanket contraindications without mechanistic explanation or practical timing guidance.
  • Fail to distinguish between blunted effects, psychiatric destabilisation, and acute toxicity.

EntheoGen aims to fill that gap with a transparent, evidence‑graded reference that is actually useful to facilitators, clinicians, and informed users.

How does it handle evidence?

The interaction model explicitly separates:

  • Tier 1 Strong formal evidence (clinical, toxicology, case reports)
  • Tier 2 Mechanistic inference (pharmacology, PK/PD, MAOI logic)
  • Tier 3 Field consensus (facilitator protocols, harm‑reduction orgs)
  • Tier 4 Recurrent anecdotal patterns (user reports)

Each interaction is tagged with classification (low risk → contraindicated), mechanism of concern, primary risk types (e.g. cardiovascular, psychiatric, blunted effects), and timing / washout notes where relevant.

Who is it for?

  • Ceremony leaders and facilitators screening participants and planning sequences.
  • Therapists and clinicians working with clients on psychiatric medications.
  • Researchers designing trials and safety protocols for psychedelic work.
  • Informed psychonauts making conservative decisions around polypharmacy.

Project status

EntheoGen is currently in private beta as part of an MSc project at the University of Exeter. The app is deployed on Microsoft Azure and the dataset is being expanded through literature review, expert elicitation, and carefully framed consultation with traditional and underground practitioners.

If you are interested in collaborating or providing expert input on specific interaction classes, please reach out via the contact details shared in the beta invitation.
