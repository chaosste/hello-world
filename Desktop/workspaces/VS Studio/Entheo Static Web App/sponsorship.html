Sponsorship & Support

EntheoGen is currently an unfunded research and harm‑reduction project. It relies on university resources, student time, and pro‑bono contributions from collaborators and subject‑matter experts.

What support would be used for

  • Expanding and curating the interaction dataset (literature access, expert honoraria).
  • Technical infrastructure (hosting, monitoring, secure data storage, CI/CD).
  • User research with facilitators, clinicians, and harm‑reduction organisations.
  • Preparing open, citable data releases and methods papers.

Who we are interested in working with

  • Academic partners (departments, centres, and labs) in psychedelics, pharmacology, or public health.
  • Harm‑reduction and psychedelic support organisations.
  • Clinics and retreat centres with strong safety culture and screening protocols.
  • Philanthropic or mission‑aligned sponsors interested in evidence‑based harm reduction.

Collaboration principles

  • Evidence‑first, with transparent grading of all sources and uncertainties.
  • Harm‑reduction focus: no promotional or “marketing” use of EntheoGen data.
  • Respect for Indigenous and traditional knowledge, including consent and co‑authorship where appropriate.
  • Open science orientation: wherever possible, outputs are published or shared under permissive licences.

Contact
